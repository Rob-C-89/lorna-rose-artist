Lorna Rose

Portrait Artist

About

I am a figurative portrait artist working in London. I produce work in oil paint or charcoal, preferring to work from life. I appreciate the versatility of oil paint in terms of mark-marking and usually working with a limited palette. I have trained in traditional methods of drawing and oil painting but like to experiment with a more contemporary style; in my self-portrait I used a palette knife to apply and remove paint to create areas lighter in value.

For me, producing a portrait is a powerful way of breaking down the social-norms between an artist and the sitter. As we work together, a familiarity forms; our individual protective mechanisms fall away and we are left in intimate connected-ness. The act of producing a portrait allows both of us to feel less isolated and the sitter to feel more seen.

Commissioned portraits start with a discussion so I can fully understand your needs and so you can understand what will be involved.

Tutoring

Contrary to popular belief, anybody can learn to draw and paint. And what's more, it doesn't have to be a tedious, technical process. Drawing and painting are fundamental human expressions and with the right guidance we can all explore everything they have to offer. As a member of my Patreon community, you will gain access to full length drawing and painting video tutorials, monthly reference images to draw or paint from and other exclusive content, covering everything from the basic principles of drawing and the exploration of different materials, colour theory and painting methods to an array of advanced techniques, concepts and ideas. Members of all levels will be able to develop their skills and love of drawing and painting.
